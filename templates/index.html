Sign of diabetes that you should know

Feeling very thristy

It happens because high levels of sugar (glucose) in the blood trigger the kidneys to work harder to filter and absorb excess sugar. When the kidneys cannot keep up, excess sugar is excreted in the urine, leading to more urination.

Blurred Vision

Blurred vision can be a symptom of diabetes because high blood sugar levels can cause changes in fluids in the body, including fluid in the eyes. High sugar levels can cause the lens of the eye to swell, which changes the eye's ability to focus, causing blurred vision.

Losing weight unintentionally

Because the body's cells don't get the glucose they need, the body starts burning fat and muscle instead for energy. This process causes rapid and unintentional weight loss, even though the person may be eating a normal amount or even more than usual.
